About CHA Cameroon

The Community Health Association (CHA) Cameroon is a non-profit organisation dedicated to improving health outcomes through community-driven programs, capacity building, and health education.

Our Mission

To promote health equity by delivering community-centred health programs that address the most critical needs of underserved populations across Cameroon — from maternal and child health to nutrition, disease prevention, and emergency response.

We work at the intersection of grassroots community mobilisation and evidence-based public health practice, partnering with government health services, international organisations, and local communities.

Through initiatives like KwataStyleMedicine, we use culturally relevant communication — including Pidgin English health education — to reach communities where they are.

Past Projects

A selection of community health projects implemented in partnership with local health facilities, community leaders, and organisations across Cameroon.

Maternal Health

Maternal Health Project — Limbe Subdivision

In partnership with Fako America Youths (FAY), CHA Cameroon implemented a targeted maternal health programme for underprivileged pregnant women in Limbe, South West Region. Services included antenatal care packages, doctor visits, laboratory tests, health talks, and follow-up support.

🕐 Completed · Fako Division, South West Region
Community Outreach

Maternal Health Field Outreach — Antenatal Campaign

CHA and FAY teams conducted structured field outreach at community health facilities, engaging healthcare workers and pregnant women in antenatal education. The campaign was driven by Cameroon's MMR of 529 per 100,000 live births — because every woman deserves a healthy pregnancy.

🕐 Completed · Limbe, Fako Division
